Small Business Resource Hub

Free tools, programs, and official resources to help your business get certified, find contracts, and grow. Curated by Rosales Law Partners.

Certification Programs

Official certification programs that open doors to set-aside government contracts.

🏛️

U.S. DOT Disadvantaged Business Enterprise (DBE) Program

The federal DBE program provides small businesses owned by socially and economically disadvantaged individuals with access to federally funded transportation contracts. Managed by the U.S. Department of Transportation.

↗ Visit Transportation.gov DBE Program
🌴

California Small Business Certification (CalCertify)

California’s official portal for SBE, DVBE, and other state certifications. Certification through CalCertify is recognized by California state agencies and many local governments.

↗ Visit CalCertify Portal
🌉

San Francisco LBE Certification (SF Office of Contract Administration)

San Francisco’s Local Business Enterprise certification provides bid preference in City contracts. Administered by the SF Office of Contract Administration (OCA).

↗ Visit SF Office of Contract Administration
🇺🇸

SBA 8(a) Business Development Program

The SBA’s 8(a) program helps small disadvantaged businesses compete for federal contracts. Participants receive business development assistance, mentoring, and access to sole-source and set-aside contracts.

↗ Visit SBA 8(a) Program
🤝

Women-Owned Small Business (WOSB) Federal Contracting Program

The WOSB program helps women-owned small businesses compete for federal contracts in industries where they are underrepresented. Certification can be obtained through SBA or approved third-party certifiers.

↗ Visit SBA WOSB Program

Finding Government Contracts

Official portals where federal, state, and local government contracts are posted.

🖥️

SAM.gov — System for Award Management

The official U.S. government portal for businesses that want to do business with the federal government. Registration in SAM.gov is required to bid on most federal contracts. You can also search for posted contract opportunities.

↗ Visit SAM.gov
📋

California eProcurement (Cal eProcure)

California’s statewide procurement portal where state agencies post solicitations for goods and services. Small businesses can register to receive notifications for contracts in their industry.

↗ Visit Cal eProcure
🏙️

San Francisco City Contracts (SF City Buy)

The City of San Francisco’s procurement portal, where City departments post solicitations for professional services, construction, and goods. LBE certified businesses receive bid preference.

↗ Visit SF City Buy

SBA Resources for Small Businesses

Free programs, training, and tools from the U.S. Small Business Administration.

💼

SBA Learning Center — Free Online Courses

Free online courses on starting a business, understanding government contracts, writing business plans, and more. Courses are self-paced and available 24/7.

↗ Visit SBA Learning Center
📞

SCORE — Free Business Mentoring

SCORE is a nonprofit network of volunteer business mentors. Small business owners can get free, confidential advice from experienced executives and entrepreneurs — in person, online, or by phone.

↗ Visit SCORE.org
🌐

SBA Small Business Development Centers (SBDC)

SBDCs provide free consulting and low-cost training to small business owners nationwide. The Bay Area SBDC network serves San Francisco and surrounding counties.

↗ Find Your Local SBDC
