Our Legal Services

We offer a full range of legal services built around the real needs of small and minority-owned businesses — from first-time certification to complex government contracts litigation.

Core Practice Areas

These are the areas where Rosales Law Partners has the deepest experience and the strongest track record.

Airports, Transit & Transportation Facilities Development

We represent private operators, small businesses, and public agencies in legal matters involving airports, municipal transit systems, and transportation facilities. Our services cover airport concessions, terminal development, federal grant compliance, security mandates, and contracts with transit agencies.

If your small business operates — or wants to operate — in an airport concession, transit system, or transportation facility, we can help you understand your rights, negotiate contracts, and stay compliant.

Talk to an Attorney

Public Law, Governmental Contracts & Procurement

Winning a government contract can transform a small business. We help you understand competitive bidding requirements, navigate RFP and RFQ processes, prepare compliant proposals, and resolve disputes when contracts are wrongly awarded to someone else.

We work across construction, engineering, professional services, and concession contracts — at the local, state, and federal level. If your business is ready to compete for public contracts, we can help you do it right.

Talk to an Attorney

Diversity, Equity & Inclusion Policy (DBE, ACDBE, SBE, LBE)

This is our signature practice area — and the one that makes the most direct difference for small business owners. We help businesses apply for and maintain DBE, ACDBE, SBE, and LBE certifications, which unlock access to billions of dollars in set-aside government contracts annually.

Our services include certification applications, compliance reviews, appeals when applications are denied, and representation in disparity studies. We also advise government agencies on designing and defending their DBE programs from constitutional challenges.
