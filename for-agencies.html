Why This Matters Now

DBE Programs Are Under Unprecedented Legal Pressure

Following SFFA v. Harvard and a wave of executive orders targeting DEI programs, government agencies that operate DBE, ACDBE, SBE, and LBE programs face more legal challenges than ever before.

The question is no longer whether your program will be challenged — it is whether your program is built to withstand one.

Rosales Law Partners has spent over 17 years on both sides of this equation: designing programs for agencies, defending them against legal attack, and advising on the federal regulations that govern what agencies can and cannot do.

How We Help Government Agencies

We provide specialized legal counsel to public agencies at every stage of diversity program design, implementation, and defense.

🛡️

Program Defense & Legal Challenges

When your DBE, ACDBE, or SBE program faces a legal challenge — contractor protest, lawsuit, or federal complaint — we step in to defend it. We build and document the legal record needed to prevail.

📐

Program Design & Compliance Review

We help agencies design or audit their diversity programs to ensure federal DOT compliance, constitutional defensibility, and alignment with current case law — before a challenge arises.

🎯

Goal-Setting & Disparity Studies

Proper goal-setting methodology is your first line of legal defense. We advise on the statistical and legal requirements for setting and documenting participation goals under federal regulations.

✈️

Airport ACDBE Programs

FAA Airport Concession DBE programs have distinct federal requirements separate from DOT DBE programs. We advise airports on ACDBE goal-setting, concession agreements, and ongoing program administration.

📜

Procurement Policy & Contract Compliance

From drafting compliant solicitation language to monitoring subcontractor participation requirements, we help agencies build procurement practices that hold up to legal scrutiny.

🎓

Staff Training & Capacity Building

We train agency procurement and legal staff on DBE program requirements, compliance obligations, and how to respond when contractors or third parties challenge program decisions.
