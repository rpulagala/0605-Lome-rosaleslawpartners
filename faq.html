Certification Programs

Questions about DBE, SBE, LBE, and other small business certification programs.

Do I need a lawyer to apply for DBE or SBE certification?

You are not legally required to have an attorney to apply, but having one significantly improves your chances of approval — and saves you time. Certification applications involve detailed documentation, personal financial disclosures, and legal analysis of ownership and control structures. Errors or missing documents are common reasons for denials. We guide clients through the entire process to make sure applications are complete and compelling from the start.

What is the difference between DBE, SBE, LBE, and M/WBE?

DBE (Disadvantaged Business Enterprise) is a federal certification for businesses owned by socially and economically disadvantaged individuals. It is required for participation in federally funded transportation contracts.

SBE (Small Business Enterprise) is a California state certification for businesses that meet size standards. It is used in many state and local procurement programs.

LBE (Local Business Enterprise) is a San Francisco-specific certification for businesses based in San Francisco. It provides preference points in SF contracting.

M/WBE (Minority/Women Business Enterprise) certifies that a business is at least 51% owned and controlled by a minority or woman. It is used in many public and private diversity contracting programs.

Many businesses qualify for more than one certification, and holding multiple certifications opens more contract opportunities. We can assess which certifications your business qualifies for.

How long does the DBE certification process take?

Processing times vary by certifying agency, but most DBE applications take 60 to 90 days after submission of a complete application. Incomplete applications can be returned for additional information, resetting the clock. Working with an attorney to submit a complete application the first time significantly reduces total wait time. Some agencies are currently experiencing longer delays due to volume — we can advise on current timelines for your specific certifying agency.

My DBE application was denied. What can I do?

A denial is not the end of the road. You have the right to appeal a DBE certification denial. The appeal process has strict deadlines — typically 90 days from the date of the denial letter — so it is important to act quickly. We have helped businesses successfully appeal denials by identifying errors in the agency’s analysis and presenting additional evidence. Contact us as soon as you receive a denial notice.

Government Contracts

Questions about competing for and winning government contracts.

What types of government contracts can my small business bid on?

Small businesses can compete for a wide range of public contracts depending on their industry, including construction, professional services, technology, concessions, transportation, and more. Set-aside contracts — reserved specifically for DBE, SBE, LBE, and other certified businesses — significantly improve your odds of winning. We help clients identify which contracts they are best positioned to win and ensure their proposals are compliant and competitive.

What is an RFP and how do I respond to one?

An RFP (Request for Proposal) is a formal document that a government agency issues when it wants to purchase services or goods. Responding to an RFP requires a detailed written proposal that addresses the agency’s requirements, demonstrates your qualifications, and outlines your pricing and approach. Proposals that do not follow the RFP instructions exactly — even minor formatting errors — are often disqualified. We review RFPs with small business clients to identify requirements, flag risks, and help craft competitive, compliant responses.
